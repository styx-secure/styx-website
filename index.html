Your words cross. Nothing follows.

Styx is an open-source, serverless, end-to-end encrypted communication platform built on Nostr and IETF Messaging Layer Security — for journalists, activists and organizations where installing a secure app is itself a risk.

01 — THE PROBLEM

Under surveillance, the app itself is evidence.

People working under authoritarian surveillance face two forms of exposure that encryption alone does not solve.

EXPOSURE Nº 1

Censorship & device inspection

Governments block secure messengers from app stores, restrict their websites and pressure centralized providers. On a confiscated device, the mere presence of a specialized privacy app attracts attention.

EXPOSURE Nº 2

Identity-linked registration

Phone numbers, emails and persistent accounts connect an encrypted conversation to a real person — through telecom records, account recovery and platform metadata.

03 — ARCHITECTURE

Standards-based cryptography, decentralized transport.

The cryptographic core is written in Rust and compiled to WebAssembly. Development is public: threat models, specifications and review gates live alongside the source.

rust → wasm
Rust core in the browser

The cryptographic core is written in Rust, compiled to WebAssembly, and runs in isolated browser workers.

ietf openmls
Standards-based MLS

IETF OpenMLS provides group key agreement and forward-secure encrypted messaging — no homegrown crypto.

nostr
Decentralized transport

Messages travel over multiple Nostr relays instead of a single centralized provider. Any relay can be swapped or self-hosted.

nip-59
Metadata protection

Gift wrapping and related techniques reduce the sender, recipient and routing metadata exposed to relays.

reproducible
Verifiable artifacts

Reproducible, integrity-verified WebAssembly builds with strengthened supply-chain verification.

versioned
Reviewable storage

Strict, versioned encrypted-storage formats designed for independent review and cross-platform compatibility.

Styx does not claim a browser can guarantee physical erasure of all memory. It follows a fail-closed design: minimal retained state, best-effort key destruction when sessions close.
